# Salvaged Sanctuary - Resisting Advancing Siege

At fifteen, the money I scraped together from a summer lifeguard job went entirely into my first computer. It lived beneath a loft bed—a cramped, subterranean desk space that felt less like bedroom furniture and more like a cave. This first *battlestation* created a cavelike enclosure—a sanctuary where the noise of the physical world dropped to zero the moment the screen lit up.

Foregoing higher education to pursue my own ambitions stripped my environment back to the bare floorboards. My new setup was built on a budget of pure necessity: a junk PC secured through an employee discount, a cheap folding table, and an exercise ball for a chair. The physical foundation was volatile, but it achieved the intended goal: a sovereign bridgehead into the net, an irreversible Rubicon built to engineer a way forward. It was unstable, but it was a means to agency.
