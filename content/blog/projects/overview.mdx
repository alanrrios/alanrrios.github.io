# Life, Liberty and the Pursuit of Happiness

> We hold these truths to be sacred & undeniable; that all men are created equal & independent, that from that equal creation they derive rights inherent & inalienable, among which are the preservation of life, & liberty, & the pursuit of happiness; …

## Some Notable Pursuits

Two things I never lacked in life were risk and ambition. These traits were notable throughout my youth and continued, albeit evolved, into adulthood. Most notably were the projects I found myself interested in and ideating to develop into full fledged endeavors.

### 2022 - Intuitive Budgeting

My one sole commitment to seeing a project through. Having spent a decade, plus plenty more now in 2026, learning and observing I took this idea from the minimal proof of concept spreadsheet it started as, to the mockup demo, to the fully interactive working demo, and having already worked and pushed through the limits of the WYSIWYG editors and platforms of before, pushing through the learning curve of full stack web development.

### 2021 - Sales Enablement

Working in sales having spent years in customer service was something of a whiplash. Customer service seems to have entirely different intents and goals of the same customer facing skillset. Although sales was nothing new to me, what with upselling product and services to offer better experiences, sales development was something new. Ultimately this led me to explore the many ways I could improve my own experience for more success, especially now that I was nearing closer to my ambitions. 

### 2020 - Air Health

Coming from the loss of a business plan that would have been completely and entirely aligned with the woes of the time I did what any versatile founder and builder would do: I pivoted on what it was that I thought made that previous ambition relevant to today's problems. What I had isolated was clean and purified air. Where [Holistic Spaces](/#holistic-spaces) was the natural breeze of the outdoors, [Air Health](#air-health) was the synthesis of healthy breathing. 

### 2019 - Holistic Spaces

Recognizing concerns around mental health and wellbeing I figured the issue being with a disconnect from reality. The "comforts" of the modern world existed as a counterintuitive ailment of our needs. One of the ways I opted to describe this concept was us as "ancient creatures living in an alien world." The idea was that our ancestors grew up in world's entirely different than the ones our bodies were inhabiting. The biological wiring that went into ancient survival instincts were being constantly hammered by perpetually alarming sensory stimuli. This was an approach social settings as holistic third-spaces to assist as a much needed remedy. 

### 2018 - Art Expression

Two years at Apple and suddenly I'm raving on about expression. The culture at Apple was strong around art and how that art is received. An example I always default to is the iPod silhouette ads. These were videos shot in three colors that showed so much energy and positivity. More than an image but emotions that show this energy anyone is capable of. 

### 2017 - Social Radio

Guy works at company known for making music devices and suddenly thinks he knows music. Although I'd never have attributed myself as DJ or anything of the sort I did have previous experience in UI/UX. The people around me had strong interests in music so, naturally, I did as well. This led me to experiment and ideate around concepts with personal radio stations. Previously, I was familiar with companies like 8tracks that were the source of many of my new favorite songs, most notably [House Of Balloons / Glass Table Girls by The Weeknd](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8ex38L8xtNI), but I thought the idea of playlists could be *better*. 

### 2016 - Social Politics

The second opportunity at voting for my future, the world I will live in, work in, love in, and it's met with so much uncertainty that my solution oriented mindset immediately starts ideating on. What was it about the voting process that I had concerns with? What was it that I thought others would have concerns with? 

What does it mean when a democracy is only as strong as its weakest voter? 

### 2015 - Messaging App

There's something about ideas born of love that *build* different. From my earliest days on the internet I grew up chatting and making friends with others on instant messaging apps. The culture and experience of having grown up chatting online and texting nonstop led to many of the designs and iterations of how I would imagine an ideal messaging app. Most importantly, it was designed around my less communicative friends. Talking with chatty chatters is one thing, the energy flows like a dance back and forth, but talking with others who aren't so caught up with the lingo can prove worrisome for all. This was, is, my love letter to encouraging conversations and discussions amongst people.

### 2014 - Blockchain Game

Not a lot to say about this one. It was a short lived idea born and ended in a weekend through a hackathon. I had a lot of developing interest in blockchain technology for what it was previously promised and known as before cryptocurrency took over as a way to pump demand for compute. This idea died with the surveyal of the threat landscape. Notable because of the recognitions born of it.

### 2013 - Web Management

My introduction into the professional world, starting and ending my career in what could have been a lucrative experience background in server management and cloud hosting, introduced me to some technology that the customers of my employer leveraged for their own businesses. Being how I wanted to be a web developer, not necessarily for developing websites for others but to know how to build a product or service offered on the web, this opportunity seemed entirely aligned with my goals. I learned a bit more about what it takes for this endeavor and figured it'd be less of a means to an end and more of a continuing responsibility that'd take away from that meant end. 

### 2012 - Social Calendar

Having moved to a new city, met new people, explored new opportunities, I thought the best thing I could do was have better ways to meet people. It was my freshman year at college and I thought there's no way social activity is this low. It's not like I was a social recluse; not exactly a social peacock but my expectations did not meet reality. My roommate was studying to be a doctor so a social *in* wasn't exactly available either. This led me to innovate on college messaging boards with the specific goal of knowing what events were available for any given week. 

## Some Notable Support

There was a time when family of close friends would entertain the idea of web development. Freelancing was all the rage and there was plenty of opportunity to be found on websites like [Fiverr](https://blog.fiverr.com/post/from-freelancing-to-founding). This, however, was entirely not aligned with the intent. It revealed more about the culture of ambition around me. Being from Texas the ambition to found a new startup at a young age was entirely contrary to societal norms. Here in the **big *blue* city** of Houston, TX the expectation was for the old oil giants to get their taste at wealth, not young new talent.

That's to say more about the culture you can expect from a "progressive" city with questionable neighbors. Whereas California was born of the American Divine Mission, Manifest Destiny, and fueled by a Gold Rush, Texas sat out in the prairie watching out for the Wild West, more bent on feeding grazing cows and sheep than fueling innovative talent.
